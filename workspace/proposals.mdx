A proposal is a pre-contract document you send to a prospective client to outline what you're offering, what it costs, and why you're the right person for the job. Unlike a contract, a proposal is not binding — it's a pitch. Once a client accepts, you can convert it into a contract and project to get work started.

<Note>
The proposals feature is currently under active development and will be available in a future update. The details on this page describe the intended functionality.
</Note>

## What proposals are for

Proposals let you present your services professionally before any formal agreement is in place. A good proposal typically covers:

- The problem or goal the client wants to address
- Your proposed approach and deliverables
- A milestone breakdown with costs
- Your timeline
- Why you're suited for this work

Hedwig uses AI to draft the proposal body based on the information you provide, so you can produce a polished document in minutes rather than hours.

## Create a proposal

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the proposals screen">
    Go to **Workspace → Proposals** and tap **New Proposal**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the proposal details">
    Provide the key information Hedwig needs to draft the proposal:

    | Field | Notes |
    |---|---|
    | Client name | The prospective client's name or company |
    | Project title | A short name for the work you're proposing |
    | Scope of work | A description of what you'll deliver |
    | Milestones | Deliverable stages with amounts and timelines |
    | Total value | The overall cost of the engagement |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the AI draft">
    Hedwig generates a professional proposal document using your inputs. Review the text and make any adjustments before sending.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send to the client">
    Enter the client's email address and tap **Send**. The client receives the proposal by email and can review it at a link.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## AI-assisted proposal generation

Hedwig's AI (powered by Google Gemini) writes the proposal narrative based on the project title, scope of work, and milestone details you provide. The generated document includes a professional introduction, a breakdown of deliverables, and a payment schedule — written in a tone suited to a client-facing pitch document.

<Tip>
Be specific when describing the scope of work. "Brand identity design including logo, color palette, and typography system" gives the AI enough context to write a compelling proposal, while "design work" does not.
</Tip>

## Convert a proposal to a contract or project

When a client accepts your proposal, you can convert it directly into a contract or a full project without re-entering any information.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Convert to contract" icon="file-text">
    Creates a binding contract pre-filled with the proposal's scope, milestones, and payment terms. Ready to send to the client for formal approval.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Convert to project" icon="folder">
    Creates a project in your workspace with the client, milestones, and budget already set up. Hedwig auto-generates a draft contract and — on Pro — draft invoices for each milestone.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
Converting a proposal does not delete it. The original proposal stays in your Proposals list so you have a record of what you pitched.
</Info>

## Proposal statuses

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Created but not yet sent |
| **Sent** | Delivered to the prospective client |
| **Viewed** | The client has opened the proposal |
| **Accepted** | The client has agreed to move forward |
| **Declined** | The client passed on the proposal |

You can track the status of all your proposals from the Proposals screen and follow up with clients who have viewed but not yet responded.
